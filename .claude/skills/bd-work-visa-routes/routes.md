## BD friction notes
- **No Dhaka consulate:** Denmark, Estonia, Luxembourg, Norway → route biometrics/decision through Delhi or a regional hub; add ~4–8 weeks + travel cost.
- **Dual-citizenship renounce-risk:** Austria, Estonia, Netherlands (verify current rule + BD DNC list). Most others permit dual citizenship — workaround if not: hold indefinite PR, keep the Bangladeshi passport.
- **Quota / lottery (fallback only):** Australia 189, Canada CRS rounds, New Zealand caps, Switzerland annual quota, US H-1B lottery.
- **Degree gate** beyond a 2022 BSc: US EB-2 NIW (needs MSc or exceptional ability), Canada CRS (favours MSc), Luxembourg/Switzerland high floors — a junior BSc may not clear.
- **English-workable, minimal language gate:** Ireland, Australia, Canada, UK, US, New Zealand. Strong B1/B2-for-citizenship burden: Germany, France, Finland, Norway, Austria (Sweden: proposed, not yet in force).

## Route-type cheat sheet
- **EU Blue Card / HSM** — degree + job offer + salary over the threshold; no labour-market test. Cleanest for a mid .NET dev who clears the floor.
- **Shortage-occupation** — IT named on the list waives the LMT (Austria RWR, Denmark Positive List, Finland Specialist, Sweden IT exemption).
- **Employer-sponsored** — employer files/nominates; watch for sponsor-licence regimes (UK, AU) that add cost/friction.
- **Points-based** — occupation list + score → fast/direct PR (AU 189, CA CRS, NZ Green List); often quota-gated.
- **Job-seeker** — enter to hunt (Germany Opportunity Card), then convert to a work permit.
- **Quota / lottery** — capped and uncertain; do not build a primary plan on it.
