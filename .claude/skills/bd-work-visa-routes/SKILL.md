# BD Work-Visa Routes

Direct skilled-work entry routes (no study-first) for a **Bangladeshi .NET engineer**, 20 first-world destinations. Snapshot **2026-06**, distilled from the `abroad-migration-info` research set.

> **Salary floors and immigration rules churn yearly. Treat every figure here as a prior, not a fact — verify live (official portal / web search) before relying on it for a decision.**

Full per-country table → `routes.md` (read on demand; don't inline it unless needed).

## Route categories
| Category | Logic | Countries |
|---|---|---|
| EU Blue Card / national HSM | salary-only, degree, no labour-market test | AT BE CZ EE FI FR DE IE IT LU NL |
| Salary-only / no-LMT | salary clears bar, no test | DE(IT-tier) IE NL NO |
| Shortage-occupation | named IT role exempts the LMT | AT DK FI SE |

| Job-seeker | enter to hunt, then convert | DE(Opportunity Card) UK(Global Talent) |
| Quota / lottery | annual cap, ranked/drawn | AU189 · CA-CRS · NZ · CH · US-H1B |

## Target shortlist (ordered by operational ease for a .NET dev — English-workability + low sponsor friction — not raw score)
- **Tier 1 — target first:** Ireland · Germany · Netherlands · Finland · Italy · New Zealand. *(Italy has the top raw score (85) but Italian-language + nulla-osta friction drops its operational ease.)*
- **Tier 2 — good:** France · Australia · Norway · Belgium · Austria · Estonia · Luxembourg.
- **Tier 3 — weak / deprioritize:** Sweden · Canada (PR is CRS-gated) · UK (£54.7k SW floor) · Denmark (DKK 552k floor) · US (H-1B lottery ~35%) · **Switzerland (quota + EU-first LMT — lowest)**.
- **Unscored:** Czechia (EU Blue Card; feasibility not yet scored — verify before targeting).

**Operational sweet spot for a mid .NET dev: Ireland & Germany** — IT on the shortage list, no sponsor-licence regime, salary floor a mid engineer clears, English-workable (Ireland fully; Germany at work, German only for fast citizenship).

## BD-specific gates (check every time)
- **Salary floor in local currency** — the most common hard gate; a junior may not clear LU / DK / CH / NL-30+.
- **IT on the official shortage list?** — if yes, the LMT is usually waived → easier sponsorship.
- **Sponsor-licence regime?** — UK CoS (+ISC fee), AU SAF levy add employer friction; DE / IE / AT have none.
- **No Dhaka consulate** (DK · EE · LU · NO) → biometrics/decision via Delhi or another hub; budget extra time + cost.
- **Dual citizenship + BD DNC** — AT / EE / NL may force renunciation on naturalisation; workaround = hold indefinite PR and keep the BD passport. Confirm the destination is on Bangladesh's Dual-Nationality-Certificate list.
- **Quota / lottery** (AU189 · CA-CRS · NZ · CH · US-H1B) → treat as fallback, not a primary route.
- **Language for PR/citizenship** — English-only: IE · UK · AU · CA · US · NZ. All others need local-language B1/B2 for PR/citizenship (per-country in `routes.md`; Sweden currently has no language test).

## Visa-friendliness scoring anchors
Feed `job-fit-scoring`'s visa dimension: **~90** Tier-1 + IT-shortage + clears floor + English-workable · **~50** sponsorship with friction (threshold/employer reluctance) · **~20** strict LMT / high floor vs pay / lottery / nationality bar.
